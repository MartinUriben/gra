Cuban Sandwich

Crispy, golden, and packed with bold flavor, the Cuban sandwich is a timeless favorite born from Cuban-American culture. This hearty pressed sandwich layers tender, juicy roast pork, sweet ham, melty Swiss cheese, tangy pickles, and zesty yellow mustard between slices of buttery Cuban bread. Grilled to perfection, each bite delivers the perfect balance of savory, salty, and tangy flavors with a satisfying crunch. Whether you're serving it for lunch, dinner, or a weekend get-together, this iconic sandwich brings big flavor with minimal fuss.

Ingredients

  • Cuban bread (or substitute with French or Italian bread if unavailable) - 1 loaf, cut in half lengthwise
  • Roast pork (sliced) - about ½ pound
  • Deli ham - about ½ pound
  • Swiss cheese - 4 to 6 slices
  • Dill pickles - thinly sliced, about ½ cup
  • Yellow mustard - to taste
  • Butter - softened, for spreading on the outside of the bread

Instructions

  1. Heat a sandwich press, panini maker, or a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Slice the Cuban bread loaf in half lengthwise (if it’s not already sliced), then cut into sandwich-sized portions if needed.
  3. Spread yellow mustard evenly on both sides of the bread.
  4. Layer the ham, roast pork, Swiss cheese, and pickles (in that order) on one half of the bread.
  5. Top with the other half of the bread.
  6. Spread softened butter on the outside (top and bottom) of the sandwich to ensure a crispy, golden crust when pressed.
  7. Place the sandwich in the panini press and press firmly.
  8. If using a skillet, place a heavy pan or foil-wrapped brick on top to press it down.
  9. Cook for about 3–5 minutes per side, or until the bread is golden and crispy, and the cheese is melted.
  10. Remove from heat, slice diagonally, and serve warm!
